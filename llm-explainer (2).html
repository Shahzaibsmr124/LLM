Introduction

So what is an LLM?

Think of it like this. You teach someone by giving them billions of books, websites and articles to read. Over time they get so good at understanding language that they can write new content on their own. That is basically what an LLM is. It reads a massive amount of text and learns how language works. Then it can write, answer questions and have conversations just like a human would.

Trillion+
Words used for training. That is basically the whole internet.
100B+
Parameters. Think of these as the model's memory and knowledge.
100+
Languages it can understand and speak fluently.
Months
That is how long it takes to fully train one of these models.
How it works

The process in 4 simple steps

You do not need to be an engineer to understand this. Here is the whole thing broken down as simply as possible.

Feed it data

The model is trained on a huge amount of text. Books, websites, Wikipedia, code and much more. We are talking trillions of words here.

Break it into pieces

The text gets chopped into small pieces called tokens. For example the word "running" might become "run" and "ning". This helps the model process things faster.

Learn what matters

The model learns which words in a sentence are important. This is called the attention mechanism and it is really the heart of how LLMs understand language.

Predict and respond

The model's job is to figure out what comes next. It gets so good at this that its responses start to feel like they came from a real person.

Real world uses

Where is it being used?

LLMs are not just chatbots. They are already changing how industries work every single day.

Software Development
Writing code, finding bugs, creating documentation and explaining what code does. LLMs handle all of it.
Real example: GitHub Copilot suggests code as developers type. Companies report 40 to 55 percent faster development speed.
Customer Support
Chatbots that work 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. They read the company's knowledge base and give accurate answers instantly.
Real example: An online store feeds in its catalog and FAQs. The chatbot handles 80 percent of questions on its own.
Document Analysis
100 page contracts, legal papers, financial reports. LLMs can read and summarize them in minutes instead of hours.
Real example: Law firms upload contracts and the LLM pulls out key clauses, risks and anything that might be missing.
Translation
More than 100 languages and it actually understands context. Not just the words but the tone and culture behind them too.
Real example: Business emails and legal text translated while keeping the brand's voice and meaning fully intact.
Research Help
Summarizing papers, writing literature reviews and coming up with new ideas. Both academic and corporate teams use this.
Real example: A scientist feeds in 50 research papers. The LLM finds the common findings and gaps in just one paragraph.
Marketing and Content
Ad copy, blog posts, product descriptions, social media content. All created at scale without losing the brand voice.
Real example: One e-commerce store generated unique descriptions for all 10,000 of its products automatically.
Watch out for these

What it cannot do

LLMs are powerful but knowing their limits is just as important as knowing what they can do.

It can make things up

Sometimes an LLM will give you wrong information but say it with full confidence. Always double check anything important before you act on it.

It has a knowledge cutoff

It does not know anything that happened after its training ended. If you need current news or live data you need to connect extra tools to it.

No memory between chats

Every new conversation starts fresh. Once you close the chat it forgets everything. It has no ongoing memory of who you are.

It can carry bias

If the training data had biases in it the model can pick those up too. For anything sensitive a human should always review the output.
